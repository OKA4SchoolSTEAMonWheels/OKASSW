About

A portable model rooted in dignity, creativity, and access.

Problem. In Kenya, about 44% of adolescents aged 10 to 17 report a recent mental health problem and 12% meet criteria for a disorder, while mental disorders are the second leading cause of disability for ages 10 to 24 according to the text currently published on the site.

Across many public and low-fee schools, young people carry emotional burdens into overcrowded, exam-driven classrooms that offer few safe spaces for reflection, making, or practical exploration.

OKA4School STEAM on Wheels exists to reach learners where they are by blending wellbeing, hands-on making, and future-focused learning through mobile circles.

Mission. To improve the wellbeing, confidence, and future-readiness of young people in under-resourced communities by turning everyday spaces into joyful, hands-on learning circles that weave together psychosocial support, baking, and accessible STEAM experiences.

Vision. A region where every learner, especially girls and those on the margins, has regular access to safe and creative spaces that nurture wellbeing, curiosity, and real pathways into STEAM and sustainable livelihoods.
