Our work

Practical, needs-aware learning support for schools and communities.

SHOFCO describes its work as split between Priorities that guide the way forward and Programs that meet real needs on the ground, and this OKA4School page follows that same structure for a mobile education and wellbeing model.

How this page works

This page separates the big-picture goals that shape OKA4School from the practical activities that deliver those goals in real schools and communities.

OKA4School STEAM on Wheels is an emerging Kenyan community-based initiative bringing mobile wellbeing, baking, and STEAM learning experiences to schools and communities, especially where resources and access are limited.

Its public story already emphasizes psychosocial support, practical baking sessions, and low-cost STEAM activities as a portable model designed to travel lightly, adapt locally, and grow through school and community partnerships.

Our priorities

Big-picture focus areas that shape where this work directs its energy.

SHOFCO frames priorities as the large goals that guide resources and long-term direction, so here the priorities reflect what is already visible in OKA4School’s public mission and vision.

Wellbeing and belonging

Young people need safer spaces for emotional support, reflection, and confidence-building alongside academic learning.

Practical, joyful learning

Baking and accessible STEAM experiences turn everyday activities into hands-on pathways for curiosity, creativity, and confidence.

Inclusive access

The initiative prioritizes learners in under-resourced schools and communities, especially girls and those on the margins.

Community-rooted partnerships

School communities, local groups, families, and future collaborators are part of how the model grows and earns trust.

Future pathways

The work links immediate learning experiences to future-readiness, self-belief, STEAM pathways, and sustainable livelihoods.

Pilot route

Start small, learn quickly, and build evidence for growth.

The current OKA4School site frames the first route as a starter pilot with a six-month shape, at least five schools, and a $500 starter goal, designed to test what works before scaling.
